ABOUT

In 1998, the NUS Climbing Club invited like-minded individuals for a day of climbing in the NUS Bouldering Gym and this invitation has reached more and more climbing enthusiasts over the years. As the number of participants grew, NUS Climbing Club and TeamNUS Climbing jointly organized a climbing event that goes beyond the NUS Bouldering Gym - Boulderactive. Inaugurated in 2001, this event became the first ever national-scale climbing-related competition, increasing the profile of bouldering in the public community and triggering zealous interest in the sport.

2020 has presented the climbing community with challenges like never before. With new restrictions and constant uncertainties, the climbing scene has had to re-adjust itself to scale these limitations. Nevertheless, here in Singapore we've seen how bouldering has picked itself up, with gyms striving to serve those who share their love for this sport through community-focused initiatives.

With BoulderActive: Reimagined, we too want to ride on the grit and success of our local gyms to not just reintroduce, but to reimagine what competition climbing can look like. Starting small, our decentralised approach holds the safety of all climbers in mind while offering an experience that isn't any less exciting - just different.

Our family here at NUS has dearly missed this large-scale event, and so has the public. We believe BoulderActive's comeback will be a meaningful one to many. With a shared desire to bring something fresh, fun and unforgettable, it is an honour that this vision can come to life - one that we hope would be a hallmark and inspiration for future climbing events.

COMPETITION FORMAT AND SCHEDULE


Regarding this year's format

Qualifiers

Climbing
  • Various routes in the climbing gym will be part of the Boulderactive set for each category and labelled accordingly.
  • Each Boulderactive route is given a predetermined point allocation (weightage).
  • Each competitor is free to attempt any Boulderactive route, scoring will be determined by only the 6 routes which grant him/her the highest number of points.
  • Only tops and zones count towards each competitor’s score. Attempts will not be scored.
  • A successful top on a route provides the full number of points allocated to that route, while obtaining a zone provides 50% of the allocated points.
  • Video verification of tops and zones must be provided in strict accordance with the rules in Annex A, failing which the Organisers retain the discretion to decline to award a competitor points for disputed tops and zones
  • The Organisers may at their discretion choose to ignore video submissions should they determine that a competitor has failed to satisfactorily adhere to the Video Submission Guidelines set out at Articles 32-35 in the Rules and Regulations.
  • For the avoidance of doubt, a competitor will not be awarded points for tops and zones on routes not part of the Boulderactive set for his/her category.
  • For avoidance of doubt, there is no limit on the number of sessions competitors are allowed to use to attempt and film the Boulderactive routes, provided video submissions are sent in before the stipulated deadline.
Submitting one's score card
  • A form will be provided via the Boulderactive Telegram channel, which all registered participants will be added to.
  • Participants are to upload their video proof into the form
  • Failure to submit by the following deadlines will result in one’s score being recorded as null
    • Novice: 13/7, 1200
    • Intermediate: 20/7, 1200
    • Opens and Teams: 28/7, 1200

Finals Format

  • Novice and Intermediate: Rotation Foramt
  • Open: IFSC concurrent format
  • Teams: NA

RULES AND REGULATIONS

Competitive category

Novice
  1. Competitors must not have been a finalist in the Novice, Intermediate or Open categories of previous competitions.
  2. National (Boulder/Lead) climbers are not allowed to join this category.
Intermediate
  1. Competitors must not have been amongst the top 3 positions in the Intermediate category of previous competitions.
  2. Competitors must not have been a finalist in the Open category of previous competitions.
  3. National (Boulder/Lead) climbers are not allowed to join this category.
Open
  1. All are welcome!
  2. National (Boulder/Lead) climbers can only join this category.
Team
  1. Each team can have a maximum of 3 people.
  2. Minimum one female and one male climber.
  3. Minimum of one Novice climber.
  4. Maximum of one Intermediate climber.
  5. National (Boulder/Lead) and Open climbers are not allowed to join this category.
* Any competitor found to have registered and competed in any category he/she is ineligible for will immediately be disqualified and moved to a higher category.
** International climbers may contact the Organisers for clarification on category eligibility.

WHY REIMAGINED?

In recent years, climbing as a sport has seen unprecedented growth, both in Singapore, and across the world. It is heartening to see the climbing community flourish and more people growing to love the sport. It remains the case, however, that competitions in Singapore remain unexplored by many who may feel inadequately prepared, and fears are further magnified with the pandemic across the community.
This year, Boulderactive seeks to remove these barriers. To do so, we have reimagined what a climbing competition looks like - hopefully just for now - while still celebrating the aspects of climbing loved by all regardless of their experience. Boulderactive 2021 brings you:

Flexibility
Our decentralised qualifiers allow climbers to compete at their convenience. With time no longer being a constraint, we’re excited to host climbers from all walks of life!

Freedom
Without the pressure of a timer or other competitors, we hope climbers regardless of experience can feel free to push their limits, unfazed and undaunted.

Community
Behind every success is a team of supporters, and our gym-based competition gives climbers the opportunity to climb in a social environment they deem most conducive - whether with friends, or by receiving moral support from their friends back home.

Safety
Last but not least, a good climb is one that takes not only the climber’s success, but also their safety, into consideration. With our safe-distancing measures and finals broadcasted post-event, we ensure that neither safety nor fun will ever be compromised. Enthusiastic competitors can climb without worrying about their health, and avid fans of the sport can cheer their favourites on from the comfort of their home.

We hope that through BA2021, every climber will come out better, wherever they are, whoever they are, whenever they climb. So join us!
